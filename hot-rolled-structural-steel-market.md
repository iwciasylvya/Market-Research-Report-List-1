<p><p>Hot Rolled Structural Steel is a type of steel formed through the hot rolling process, which involves heating steel above its recrystallization temperature and then shaping it into desired forms such as beams, channels, and angles. Hot rolled structural steel is widely used in building frameworks, bridges, and heavy machinery due to its high strength-to-weight ratio and cost-effectiveness.</p><p>The Hot Rolled Structural Steel Market is experiencing significant growth, driven by increasing demand from the construction and infrastructure sectors, particularly in emerging economies.
