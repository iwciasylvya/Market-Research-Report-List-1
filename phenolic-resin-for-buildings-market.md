<p><p>Phenolic resins are synthesized polymers widely used in building applications due to their durability and resistance to heat and chemicals. The market can be categorized into two main types: thermoplastic phenolic resin, which becomes pliable when heated and can be reshaped, making it ideal for applications requiring flexibility; and thermosetting phenolic resin, which hardens permanently upon curing, offering exceptional strength and stability. Both types play critical roles in insulation, adhesives, and composite materials within the construction industry.</p></p>
